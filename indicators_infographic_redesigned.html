Dataset

Landsat Collection 2 Tier 1 Level 2 8-Day NDVI Composite

Methodology

Computed by determining reflectance values in visible red (RED) and near-infrared (NIR) bands of remotely sensed images. Values calculated per village by averaging pixel values of the NDVI raster within each village boundary.

Rationale

The greenness of the environment had a positive effect on pregnancy outcomes, despite poor air quality and civilization (24). Increasing evidence has shown that exposure to greenness in the vicinity of home is related to health (25). Previous research has shown correlation between satellite derived vegetation indices such as NDVI with health wellbeing as a proxy measure for air quality (26‚Äì28). High NDVI values are inversely proportional to air pollution levels.

Rationale

Dataset

Road dataset, HDX population data (100m resolution)

Methodology

RQI = (Œ£ (road length √ó speed limit) / Œ£ road length) / 120 km/h

Rationale

The quality of roads used to transport women to health facilities differ with the type of the road and seasonal variations. Paved or unpaved roads affects the quality of the road which affects navigability. During the wet season, roads are flooded with poor quality due to flooding and slippery surfaces. These limitations affect speed limit in each respective road surface. Travel times to seek healthcare on roads with low RQI are long and may have adverse health outcomes (39).

Methodology

Spatial Access to Care = (Œ£ (Population_i √ó Access_i)) / (Œ£ Population_i) Where: Population_i = Population of grid cell i Access_i = Travel distance and time from grid cell centroid to nearest healthcare facility Calculated using network analysis, averaged per community.
