Population-Weighted Methodology Example

Environmental exposures are calculated using Carto's H3 hexagonal tessellation to account for heterogeneous population distribution within village boundaries. Each metric is weighted by the population residing in each grid cell, ensuring that densely populated areas have proportionally greater influence on the village-level estimate.

Dataset

Landsat Collection 2 Tier 1 Level 2 8-Day NDVI Composite; MODIS - MOD09GA/Q1

Methodology

Residential greenness has been consistently associated with improved pregnancy outcomes and overall population health. NDVI leverages the differential reflectance of vegetation in the visible red (RED) and near-infrared (NIR) spectral bands, with higher values indicating denser and healthier vegetation cover and, indirectly, lower levels of ambient air pollution.

NDVI = (NIR - RED) / (NIR + RED) Village-level greenness was quantified by spatially averaging NDVI values within each village boundary.

Rationale

Satellite-derived vegetation indices, particularly NDVI, are widely used as proxy measures of environmental quality and exposure to air pollution. Higher values indicate denser and healthier vegetation cover.

Dataset

Methodology

Residential proximity to major roadways is commonly used as a proxy for traffic-related air pollution exposure. Epidemiological evidence indicates that women residing within close proximity (e.g., less than 200m) to major roads experience an increased risk of adverse pregnancy outcomes, including preterm birth.

Distance_highway = Sum(Population_i x Distance_i) / Sum(Population_i) where Distance_i represents the Euclidean distance from grid cell i to the nearest highway.

Rationale

Pollutants produced by motorized vehicles, such as CO, NOx, HC, SO2, Pb and CO2, have negative impacts on the body. Traffic-related pollutant concentrations exhibit a strong spatial gradient with non-linear decay with increasing distance.

Methodology

RH calculated using Magnus-Tetens approximation: e_s(T) = exp((a * T)/(b + T)) RH = 100 x (e_s(T_dew) / e_s(T_air)) where a=17.625, b=243.04
